Foundational Stage Curriculum

At GM Legacy School, the Foundational Stage forms the most crucial phase of a child's educational journey. This stage lays the foundation for lifelong learning by nurturing language, thinking, social skills, creativity, and emotional well-being in a joyful and stress-free environment.

The Foundational Stage is designed in accordance with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, which emphasises play-based, activity-based, and experiential learning for children between the ages of 3 and 8 years.

Aims of the Foundational Stage

The Foundational Stage aims to:

Language & Communication

Develop strong language and communication skills

Foundational Literacy & Numeracy

Build foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN)

Social & Emotional Development

Promote social, emotional, and moral development

Curiosity & Creativity

Encourage curiosity, creativity, and imagination

Physical Development

Support physical development and motor skills

Confidence & Independence

Build confidence, independence, and positive learning habits

Key Learning Areas

1

Language Development

  • Listening and speaking skills
  • Story-telling, rhymes, songs, and conversation
  • Early reading and writing
  • Introduction to mother tongue and English
2

Foundational Literacy and Numeracy

  • Letter recognition and phonics
  • Number sense, counting, and basic operations
  • Shapes, patterns, and measurement
  • Development of logical thinking
3

Environmental Awareness

  • Understanding self, family, school, and surroundings
  • Nature, plants, animals, and seasons
  • Simple concepts of health, hygiene, and safety
4

Creative Expression

  • Art and craft activities
  • Music, dance, and movement
  • Role play, drama, and storytelling
5

Physical Development

  • Fine motor skills through drawing, cutting, and writing
  • Gross motor skills through games, yoga, and outdoor play
  • Development of coordination and balance
6

Socio-Emotional Development

  • Sharing, cooperation, and empathy
  • Self-awareness and emotional expression
  • Respect for others and classroom discipline

Teaching–Learning Approach

Play-based learning as the core method

Learning through stories, games, songs, activities, and exploration

Child-centred and activity-oriented classrooms

Flexible pace according to each child's readiness

Teachers act as facilitators and caregivers, guiding children gently and patiently.

Assessment in the Foundational Stage

  • No formal written examinations in early years
  • Assessment through continuous observation, activities, portfolios, and oral work
  • Focus on growth, not grading
  • Regular feedback shared with parents

Learning Environment

Safe & Child-friendly

Safe, colourful, and child-friendly classrooms

Age-appropriate Furniture

Age-appropriate furniture and learning materials

Learning Corners

Learning corners for reading, play, art, and exploration

Safety & Security

Emphasis on hygiene, safety, and emotional security

Parental Partnership

Parents are treated as partners in early learning. The school:

  • Conducts regular parent–teacher interactions
  • Shares progress through reports and meetings
  • Guides parents on supporting learning at home

Our Commitment

At GM Legacy School, the Foundational Stage curriculum ensures that every child:

Learns with joy and confidence

Develops strong foundations in literacy and numeracy

Grows in character, creativity, and curiosity

We believe that a strong beginning creates a strong future.

Nurturing young minds with excellence, integrity, and innovation for a brighter tomorrow.
