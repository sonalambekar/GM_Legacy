Primary Curriculum

At GM Legacy School, the Primary Curriculum builds upon the strong foundation laid in the early years and focuses on developing core academic skills, learning habits, values, and confidence in children.

The Primary Stage corresponds mainly to the Preparatory Stage of NEP 2020 and the early part of the Middle Stage, typically covering children between the ages of 6 and 11 years.

Our primary education programme aims to make learning meaningful, joyful, and skill-oriented, while steadily preparing students for subject-based learning in higher classes.

Aims of the Primary Curriculum

The Primary Curriculum aims to:

Strong Literacy & Numeracy

Develop strong literacy and numeracy skills

Conceptual Understanding

Build conceptual understanding in all subjects

Critical Thinking

Foster curiosity, creativity, and critical thinking

Study Habits

Cultivate good study habits and self-discipline

Values & Social Skills

Promote values, social skills, and positive behaviour

Independence

Encourage independence and responsibility in learning

Core Subject Areas

1

Languages

  • Mother Tongue / Regional Language
  • English
  • Emphasis on listening, speaking, reading, and writing
  • Vocabulary building, grammar, comprehension, and creative writing
2

Mathematics

  • Number operations and number sense
  • Fractions, decimals, and measurement
  • Geometry, patterns, and data handling
  • Development of logical reasoning and problem-solving skills
3

Environmental Studies (EVS) / Science & Social Awareness

  • Understanding family, community, and environment
  • Basic concepts of plants, animals, weather, and health
  • Introduction to history, geography, and civics
  • Environmental education and sustainability
4

Arts Education

  • Drawing, painting, and craft
  • Music, dance, and drama
  • Creative expression and appreciation of arts
5

Physical Education & Health

  • Games, sports, yoga, and fitness activities
  • Development of motor skills, stamina, and teamwork
  • Awareness of health, hygiene, and nutrition
6

Digital Literacy & Life Skills

  • Basic computer skills and safe use of technology
  • Communication skills, teamwork, and problem-solving
  • Values education and citizenship

Teaching–Learning Approach

Activity-based and experiential learning

Learning through projects, experiments, group work, and discussions

Integration of arts and sports with academics

Use of teaching aids, smart classrooms, and digital resources

Child-centred classrooms with individual attention

Teachers encourage students to ask questions, explore ideas, and think independently.

Assessment in the Primary Stage

  • Combination of formative and periodic assessments
  • Evaluation through worksheets, projects, oral work, and tests
  • Focus on understanding and application, not memorisation
  • Regular feedback shared with parents through report cards and PTMs

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the Primary Stage, students are expected to:

Reading & Writing

Read and write with fluency and comprehension

Mathematical Skills

Perform mathematical operations with confidence

Scientific Understanding

Understand basic scientific and social concepts

Communication

Communicate effectively and work collaboratively

Character

Demonstrate discipline, responsibility, and ethical behaviour

Parental Involvement

The school believes in a strong home–school partnership. Parents are encouraged to:

  • Support regular reading and homework habits
  • Attend parent–teacher meetings
  • Participate in school activities and programmes

Our Commitment

At GM Legacy School, the Primary Curriculum ensures that every child:

Develops strong academic foundations

Learns with interest and confidence

Grows in values, skills, and character

We prepare our primary students not only for the next class, but for a lifetime of learning.

GM Legacy School is dedicated to providing quality education and nurturing young minds to become responsible citizens and future leaders.
